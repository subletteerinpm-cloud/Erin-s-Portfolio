Fashion Report 2026: Marketing Landing Page for AI-Powered Fashion Insights

Designed and built a conversion-focused marketing landing page to showcase AI-driven fashion analytics, drive newsletter signups, and generate demo leads—achieving Page 1 SEO rankings and strong early engagement.

Role: Product Designer — Strategy, Visual Design, Webflow Build

Timeline: 2026

Deliverables: Visual hierarchy and content strategy, v0 prototype, production design and build in Webflow

The Challenge

Fashion Report 2026 is an AI-powered fashion insights product. The team had existing webinar and slide content that communicated the value proposition, but it was not built for the web: dense slides, text-heavy layouts, and weak visual hierarchy made it hard for visitors to quickly grasp key stats and differentiators.

The goal was to turn that material into a dedicated marketing landing page that could rank on search, capture leads, and book demos—without losing the substance of the original narrative. The core question: How might we transform webinar-style content into a scannable, stats-first web experience that builds trust and drives action?

Before: Existing Materials & Early Exploration

My Approach

Content & Hierarchy

I audited the webinar deck and slide content to extract the core narrative and key statistics. The story needed to work in a modular, scroll-based format: hero value proposition, proof points and stats, feature or benefit cards, and clear CTAs for newsletter signup and demo booking. Emphasis shifted from long paragraphs to scannable headlines and stat callouts.

Design Principles

Stats-first visual hierarchy Lead with numbers and outcomes so visitors grasp value in seconds
Card-based architecture Modular sections and cards for flexible, responsive layout and future updates
Conversion clarity Clear primary and secondary CTAs with consistent placement and styling

Process

I used v0 to rapidly prototype a card-based layout and stats-first structure, then refined the design in Figma for spacing, typography, and responsive behavior. The final build was implemented in Webflow with responsive grids, hover states, and form integration for newsletter and demo requests.

The Solution

After: Prototype to Production

What I Learned

Webinar content needs a web-native translation. Slides are built for a presenter; landing pages are built for scanning. Extracting the narrative and rebuilding it with stats-first hierarchy and modular cards made the same message much more effective online.

v0 accelerated structure, not just visuals. Using v0 to lock in card-based architecture and stat emphasis early gave stakeholders a shared reference and made the Figma and Webflow phases faster and more focused.

Refined spacing and states matter at launch. Moving from prototype to production in Webflow—refining spacing, responsive breakpoints, and hover states—had a clear impact on perceived polish and conversion clarity.
