MorningAI: Reimagining the Content Creation Experience

Redesigning an AI-powered marketing platform to reduce decision paralysis and accelerate content creation for small marketing teams.

Role: Lead UX Designer — Research, Strategy, Interaction Design, Prototyping

Timeline: January 2026

Deliverables: Competitive analysis, workflow redesign, design specifications, interactive prototypes

The Challenge

MorningAI is an autonomous growth platform that helps small marketing teams compete with larger brands by using AI to generate campaigns, social posts, emails, and more.

The problem: The existing content creation flow required users to navigate through 9 nested category tabs (Image & Video, Social Media, Ads, Sales Enablers, Blog, Email, Public Relations, Strategy, Blank) before they could create anything. Users faced decision paralysis at the first screen, abandoning briefs before generating content.

The core question: How might we transform a complex, wizard-based creation flow into an intuitive experience that gets marketers from idea to published content faster?

Before: The Original Experience

My Approach

Research & Discovery

Competitive Analysis: I evaluated leading AI content tools (Copy.ai, Jasper, Writesonic, and others) to map common patterns in creation flows, command interfaces, and dashboard layouts. Key insight: tools that prioritized natural language input over category selection saw higher completion rates and faster time-to-first-output.

Stakeholder Input: Interviews with product leadership, sales, and customer success revealed recurring themes—users loved the AI output quality but found the upfront workflow intimidating. Support tickets frequently cited "too many choices" and "not sure where to start."

Strategic Framework

Chat Creates, Canvas Edits Replace the 9-category wizard with a natural language command bar
One Canvas, Not Three Pages Consolidate the Brief → Edit → Share flow
Progressive Disclosure Start simple, reveal complexity only as needed

Design Solutions

Dashboard Redesign

  • “For You” personalized surface replacing category-first navigation
  • Quick-action prompts based on recent activity and campaigns
  • Reduced cognitive load by surfacing next steps instead of all options

Content Creation Canvas

  • Single workspace for brief input, generation, editing, and sharing
  • Inline editing with AI-assisted refinements
  • Contextual export options (e.g., schedule, copy, download)

Enhanced Chat Interface

  • Command bar with suggested prompts and templates
  • Expert AI personas for specialized assistance (e.g., social, email, ads)
  • Streamlined conversation history for iterative refinement

The Solution

What I Learned

Reduce before you add. The most impactful changes came from removing friction—collapsing nine categories into a command bar, merging three pages into one—rather than layering on new features. Sometimes the best design is subtraction.

Natural language is the new navigation. Users increasingly expect to describe what they want rather than hunt through menus. Tools that embrace conversational input can skip the taxonomy problem entirely.

Progressive disclosure builds confidence. Starting with simple prompts and revealing advanced options as users gain familiarity helped reduce overwhelm while preserving power-user workflows.
